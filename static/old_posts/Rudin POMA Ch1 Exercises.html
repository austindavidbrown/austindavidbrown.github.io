Prove $\sup{A} + \sup{B} = \sup{A + B}$. Clearly, $\sup{A} + \sup{B}$ is an upper bound for $A + B$. To prove that it is a least upper bound, let $\epsilon > 0$. Then since $\sup{A}$, there exists an $a \in A$ such that $\sup{A} - \epsilon / 2 < a$, and since $\sup{B}$, there exists a $b \in B$ such that $\sup{B} - \epsilon / 2 < b$. Hence, $\sup{A} + \sup{B} - \epsilon < a + b \in S$ for some $a \in A$ and some $b \in B$. By this characterization, we are done. $\Box$ References.
