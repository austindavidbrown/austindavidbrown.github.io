Local Maximum. If $f$ is a real map on the metric space X then $p \in X$ is a local maximum iff $\exists \delta : \forall p, d(p, q) < \delta \implies f(p) \le f(q)$ Theorem. Interior Extremum. Let $f : [a, b] \to \mathbb{R}$, and if $f$ has a local maximum at $x \in (a, b)$, then $f'(x) = 0$.
