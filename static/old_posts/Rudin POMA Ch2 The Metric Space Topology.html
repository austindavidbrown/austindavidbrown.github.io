Now, for a bunch of definitions. Section. Open subsets. Definition. An open ball is defined $B_r(x) = \{ y \in X : d(y, x) < r \}$. Definition. The set $S$ is open iff for all $x \in S$, there exists a $B_r(x) \subset S$. Theorem. The metric forms a topology on the metric space X. (i) $\mathbb{R}$, $\emptyset$ are open. Proof. These are open by definition. (ii) For any family of open sets $\{ S_i \}_{\alpha \in A}$, $\bigcup_{\alpha \in A} S_\alpha$ is open. Proof. For all $x \in S_{\alpha}$ for some $\alpha$, there exists a $B_r(x) \subset S_{\alpha} \subset \bigcup_{\alpha \in A} S_\alpha$. $\Box$ (iii) For any finite family of open sets $\{ S_i \}_{1 \le i \le n}$, $\bigcap_{1 \le i \le n} S_i$ is open.
